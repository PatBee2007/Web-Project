Hades

Intro Hades PvZ Undertale

Ancient Greece has brought about many tales to tell, the twelve great labours of Heracles, the Oddyssey of Oddeseus, Theseus and the Minotaur, and even nowadays, one has still been told, of an espace from the Underworld by the son of Hades, Zagreus.

Hades is a roguelike game, where the player, Zagreus, must fight through the various locations of the Greek underworld in an attempt to get out and find his mother, facing various different Underworld denizens while getting all sorts of different power-ups.

Speaking of, you may be asking the question “what is a roguelike?” and, simply put, it’s a dungeon explorer where you go through different runs, upgrading yourself as you go, making different choices on how to upgrade yourself, and upon death, restarting at the beginning to try again, the main thing about them is that no two runs will ever be completely the same, the order of enemies you fight and the options given to you are always changing, especially since you yourself have 6 different options of weapons to pick before you start a run, each of them having four different versions of themselves to pick from.

As for all the specific rewards you can get, these are claimed everytime you clear an encounter, before choosing which reward from the choice of rooms available to go into will be more beneficial, and entering the next encounter, there’s plenty of different rewards to choose from, treasures like Darkness and Gemstones can be used to upgrade stuff back at the starting area for either cosmetics or to improve future runs, Obol is used to buy stuff in the shops, and Centaur Hearts increase your max health, but the biggest rewards of runs are the Boons from your Olympian relatives, different gods who all grant different effects to your regular attacks, Zeus boons summon lightning bolts for your attacks, Poseidon’s make waves to knock foes away, Ares causes an immediate piece of additional damage, while Dionysus gives foes a hangover to damage over time, Hermes speeds up you and your attacks while Demeter slows foes down with frost effects, Athena boosts your defence and deflects attacks, Aphrodite weakens foes, and Artemis gives a chance to double your attack with Critical strikes. And all of that is without mentioning Daedalus Hammer, which grants new abilities to each of your weapons.

All of this honestly barely scratches the surface of just how many different variations your combat style can be given, there's so many combinations of different Boons and weapons to choose from, and it's all necessary for some of the very difficult fights ahead, dosens of different denizens of the Underworld each with different methods of attacking you, be it charging, lobbing from a range, poisoning you, and even those who can block attacks, or summon more foes to fight you, and that's just the normal ones, bosses like the Furies, a Bone Hydra, a King with his own godly boons working beside The Minotaur, and even Zagreus' own father, Hades, God of the dead all stand in your way to stop you.

Of course, while the combat is the main part of the gameplay, the best part is all the fun characters each with their own personalities that you interact with and grow your relationship with by handing out bottles of Nectar and Ambrosia, as well as other, more specific stuff that play out various side stories, from helping Hades reunite with his estranged wife and his godly relatives, helping Orpheus and Achilles find their old loves, and Zagreus forming his own love with Megaera and Thanatos, or just, improving your relationship with friends, family and Hellhound Cerberus in general, with 25 people for you to max out a relationship with.

Ultimately, there's plenty to love about Hades, and I'd definitely recommend checking it out.
